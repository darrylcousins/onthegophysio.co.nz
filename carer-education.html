Carer Education

In the 2006 New Zealand Census 420 000 people were acting as 'carers'. Providing love and assistance to a spouse, family member or friend is often done naturally and usually without formal training. If the care provided involves physically assisting a person to get up from a chair, out of bed, in and out of the car, or even just offering an arm to steady them as they walk, then it is important the 'carer' knows how to keep themselves and the person they care for safe. On the Go Physio can provide one on one training at home, or group education sessions, to support carers and decrease the risk of accidental harm.

One on one training at home

On the Go Physio offers an in depth, home based assessment of how a person is physically managing and what assistance is provided.The person/people providing the care are then taught the safest way to carry out each task. This can be done in a single session or spread out over a few visits depending on the needs of the clients. If a family is sharing the caring role then we can provide photographic charts so each person is consistent.

Group training sessions

These sessions are designed to be run for health and disability organisations that offer education and support for carers. Usually the organisation hosting the session would provide a venue suitable to the number of participants. Courses can be tailored to meet the needs of the group. The standard workshops are...

Handle With Care

2 hours

This covers a range of situations in which a person may provide physical assistance to someone they care for. The course is completely practical and includes

  • "where to put your hands" - basic principles of safely assisting somebody to keep them and you safe
  • Helping somebody up from a chair
  • Helping somebody in and out of a car
  • Helping somebody who is unsteady as they walk
  • Helping somebody get up from the ground

Dealing With Falls

1-1/2 hours

This course is ideal for people who are caring for somebody who has fallen before or is at risk of falling. It includes.

  • How to decrease the risk of falling – practical steps you can take to minimise risk
  • How to safely help a person up from the floor when they are uninjured

This is also a practical course and all participants will have the opportunity to practise until they are confident in judging when it is safe to assist a person up and how to do it.

All our courses are based on the current 'New Zealand Patient Handling Guidelines'.

Fit To Care

1 hour

Often people who are caring for others put their own health needs last. This course focuses on how carers can look after their own physical health and what a balanced physical activity programme comprises. It involves both educational and practical components.
